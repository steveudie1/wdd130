Abijan

Abidjan, founded in 1898 on the Ébrié Lagoon as a French fishing village, became the capital of Ivory Coast in 1934, replacing Bingerville. It evolved into a major economic hub, solidified by the 1951 construction of the Vridi Canal. While the capital shifted to Yamoussoukro in 1983, Abidjan remains the country's economic, financial, and industrial center.
